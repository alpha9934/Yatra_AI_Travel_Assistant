YATRA CORPORATE TRAVEL POLICY v2.1

=== SECTION 1: FLIGHT POLICY ===
1.1 CLASS OF TRAVEL
All employees must travel economy class for domestic flights.
Business class is permitted only when flight duration exceeds 6 hours or for C-suite VP and above employees.
Premium economy is not reimbursable unless explicitly approved.

1.2 BOOKING LEAD TIME
Flights must be booked at least 3 business days before departure.
Last-minute bookings under 3 days require written approval from the reporting manager.
Emergency same day bookings require Department Head approval.

1.3 PREFERRED AIRLINES
Preferred carriers in order: Air India, IndiGo, Vistara.
Non-preferred carriers allowed but must be cost-justified.

1.4 FARE DEVIATION
Booked fare must not exceed lowest available fare by more than 20 percent.
Differences above 20 percent require manager approval.

=== SECTION 2: HOTEL POLICY ===
2.1 NIGHTLY RATE LIMITS
Tier-1 cities Mumbai Delhi Bengaluru Hyderabad Chennai Pune: Maximum 6000 rupees per night.
Tier-2 cities all other domestic: Maximum 4000 rupees per night.
International: Typical cap USD 150 per night.

2.2 PREFERRED HOTELS
Marriott, ITC Hotels, Taj Hotels are preferred chains.

2.3 LOYALTY POINTS
Employees may retain personal loyalty points earned on company-paid stays.

=== SECTION 3: MEAL ALLOWANCE ===
3.1 DOMESTIC ALLOWANCE
Up to 800 rupees per day for domestic travel.
Receipts required for individual meals exceeding 300 rupees.

3.2 INTERNATIONAL ALLOWANCE
Up to 1500 rupees per day or equivalent in local currency.

3.3 CLIENT ENTERTAINMENT
Maximum 1200 rupees per person for client meals.
Pre-approval required if group exceeds 5 people or total exceeds 10000 rupees.

3.4 ALCOHOL
Alcohol is strictly NOT reimbursable under any circumstances including client entertainment.

3.5 MINIBAR
Minibar charges are NOT reimbursable.
Room service is reimbursable as part of daily meal allowance.

=== SECTION 4: GROUND TRANSPORT ===
4.1 TAXIS
Uber and Ola cabs reimbursable with receipt.
Surge pricing reimbursable up to 2x standard fare only.

4.2 PERSONAL VEHICLE
Reimbursed at 8 rupees per kilometre. Odometer readings required.

4.3 AIRPORT TRANSFERS
Airport pickup and drop reimbursable for all business trips.

4.4 PUBLIC TRANSPORT
Metro and public transport fully reimbursable. No receipt required under 50 rupees.

=== SECTION 5: APPROVAL THRESHOLDS ===
5.1 Under 15000 rupees: Self-approved.
5.2 Between 15000 and 50000 rupees: Reporting manager approval required.
5.3 Above 50000 rupees: Department Head approval required.
5.4 International travel: Always requires Department Head plus HR approval regardless of cost.
5.5 Last-minute bookings: Always require Department Head approval regardless of cost.

=== SECTION 6: EXPENSE SUBMISSION ===
6.1 DEADLINE
Submit expenses within 30 days of travel.
Late 30 to 60 days requires manager approval.
Beyond 60 days not reimbursed without CFO exception.

6.2 RECEIPTS
Receipt required for all expenses above 500 rupees.
Self-declaration sufficient for expenses under 500 rupees.

6.3 REIMBURSEMENT
Approved claims processed within 7 business days.
Rejected claims returned with reason within 3 business days.

=== SECTION 7: DISRUPTION ===
7.1 FLIGHT DELAYS
Delays 2 hours or more: meal voucher up to 400 rupees reimbursable.
Delays 4 hours or more: rebook on next available flight at no personal cost.
Overnight delay: hotel up to 5000 rupees per night reimbursable.

7.2 CANCELLED FLIGHTS
Full refund or next flight at airline cost.
Additional hotel and meals due to airline cancellation fully reimbursable.

7.3 MISSED FLIGHTS
Employee negligence: rebooking not reimbursable.
Force majeure or connecting delay: full rebooking support provided.

=== SECTION 8: NON-REIMBURSABLE ITEMS ===
The following are never reimbursable:
Alcohol and tobacco.
Personal entertainment movies spa gym.
Minibar charges.
Traffic fines and parking violations.
Personal shopping.
Family or spouse travel costs.
Declined trip insurance.

=== SECTION 9: INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL ===
9.1 VISA AND DOCUMENTATION
Visa fees are fully reimbursable.
Passport renewal required for business travel is reimbursable once every 10 years.

9.2 FOREIGN EXCHANGE
Forex cards issued through corporate account are preferred.
Personal credit card forex charges reimbursable at actual exchange rate on transaction date.

9.3 INTERNATIONAL PHONE
International roaming pack up to 3000 rupees per week is reimbursable.
Personal calls during international travel are not reimbursable.

=== SECTION 10: COMPLIANCE AND VIOLATIONS ===
10.1 First violation: Warning issued and expense claim denied for non-compliant portion.
10.2 Repeat violations: Formal written warning added to HR record.
10.3 Serious violations: Fraudulent claims may result in termination.
10.4 Whistleblowing: Report fraud to ethics at yatra-corp.com. All reports are confidential.

=== FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ===

Q: Can I book business class for a 4 hour domestic flight?
A: No. Business class is only permitted for flights exceeding 6 hours duration or for VP and above employees.

Q: What is the hotel limit in Bangalore?
A: Bengaluru is a Tier-1 city. The maximum reimbursable hotel rate is 6000 rupees per night.

Q: Is alcohol reimbursable during client dinners?
A: No. Alcohol is strictly not reimbursable under any circumstances, including client entertainment.

Q: How many days do I have to submit my expenses?
A: You must submit all expenses within 30 days of trip completion. Late submissions require manager approval.

Q: Do I need approval for a 20000 rupee trip?
A: Yes. Trips costing between 15000 and 50000 rupees require reporting manager approval before booking.

Q: What happens if my flight is delayed by 3 hours?
A: You are entitled to a meal voucher up to 400 rupees. If delay exceeds 4 hours you may rebook on the next available flight.

Q: Can I keep hotel loyalty points earned on business trips?
A: Yes. Employees may retain personal loyalty points earned on company-paid hotel stays.

Q: Is Uber surge pricing reimbursable?
A: Surge pricing is reimbursable up to 2x the standard fare. Anything above 2x is at your own expense.

Q: What is the meal allowance for international travel?
A: Up to 1500 rupees per day or equivalent in local currency. Original receipts required for all amounts.

Q: Do I need approval for international travel?
A: Yes. All international travel regardless of cost requires Department Head approval plus HR sign-off.
