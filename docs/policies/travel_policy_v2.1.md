# YATRA CORPORATE TRAVEL POLICY
## Version 2.1 — FY2025
### Effective Date: April 1, 2025

---

## SECTION 1 — FLIGHTS

### 1.1 Class of Travel
All employees must travel economy class for domestic flights. Business class is permitted only under the following conditions:
- Flight duration exceeds 6 hours (measured gate-to-gate)
- Employee is C-suite level (VP and above)

Economy class upgrades using personal loyalty points are permitted at the employee's expense.

### 1.2 Advance Booking
All flights must be booked at least 3 business days in advance. Emergency exceptions require prior written approval from the reporting manager.

### 1.3 Preferred Airlines
Yatra has negotiated corporate rates with the following carriers, listed in order of preference:
1. Air India (primary preferred carrier)
2. IndiGo
3. Vistara

Employees should book with preferred airlines whenever the fare difference does not exceed 20% above the lowest available fare. Booking a non-preferred carrier must be documented with a fare comparison.

### 1.4 Fare Policy
Maximum allowable fare deviation from the lowest available fare at time of booking: 20%. Booking a higher fare requires manager justification in the expense system.

---

## SECTION 2 — HOTELS

### 2.1 Nightly Rate Limits
Hotel accommodation limits per night (including taxes):
- Tier-1 cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune): ₹6,000/night
- Tier-2 cities (all other cities): ₹4,000/night
- International travel: As per destination-specific guidelines issued by HR

### 2.2 Preferred Hotel Chains
Yatra has corporate accounts with the following hotel chains:
- Marriott (corporate rate applies automatically with booking reference)
- ITC Hotels
- Taj Hotels & Resorts

Preferred hotels should be booked whenever available within the rate limit.

### 2.3 Loyalty Points
Employees may retain hotel loyalty points earned during business travel for personal use. This is a benefit of the Yatra corporate travel program.

### 2.4 Extended Stays
For stays exceeding 7 nights, employees must seek approval from the department head. Service apartments may be considered as a cost-effective alternative.

---

## SECTION 3 — MEALS AND DAILY ALLOWANCES

### 3.1 Domestic Meal Allowance
The daily meal allowance for domestic travel is ₹800 per day. This covers all meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner). Receipts required for claims above ₹500 per meal.

### 3.2 International Meal Allowance
The daily meal allowance for international travel is ₹1,500 per day (or equivalent in local currency at the RBI rate on the date of expense).

### 3.3 Client Entertainment
Meals with clients are reimbursable up to ₹1,200 per person. Prior approval from the department head is required for groups exceeding 5 people. The business purpose and list of attendees must be documented.

### 3.4 Alcohol Policy
Alcohol is NOT reimbursable under any circumstances. This applies to all expense categories including client entertainment meals. Any bill that includes alcohol must be separated; the food/non-alcoholic portion may be claimed.

### 3.5 Mini-Bar and Room Service
Room service is reimbursable within the daily meal allowance. Mini-bar charges are NOT reimbursable.

---

## SECTION 4 — GROUND TRANSPORT

### 4.1 App-Based Cabs
Uber, Ola, and other app-based cab services are fully reimbursable with digital receipt. Employees should use economy/standard tier (UberGo, Ola Mini).

### 4.2 Auto-Rickshaw
Auto-rickshaw fares are reimbursable up to ₹200 per journey. Fare above ₹200 requires a receipt.

### 4.3 Personal Vehicle
Use of personal vehicle for business travel is reimbursable at ₹8 per kilometer. Employees must submit the odometer reading (start and end) along with the expense claim. Fuel receipts are not required separately.

### 4.4 Airport Transfers
Pickup and drop to/from airport for business travel is reimbursable. Preferred: app-based cabs (Uber/Ola). Pre-booked airport shuttles or radio cabs are also acceptable.

### 4.5 Rental Cars
Car rentals require prior approval from the department head. Fuel for rental cars is reimbursable with receipts. Traffic penalties and fines are NOT reimbursable.

---

## SECTION 5 — APPROVAL THRESHOLDS

### 5.1 Domestic Travel Approvals
- Total trip cost below ₹15,000: Employee self-approves in the system
- Total trip cost ₹15,000 – ₹50,000: Requires reporting manager approval (within 2 business days)
- Total trip cost above ₹50,000: Requires department head approval (within 3 business days)

### 5.2 International Travel Approvals
All international travel, regardless of cost, requires:
1. Department head written approval
2. HR Business Partner approval
3. Finance sign-off for trips exceeding ₹2,00,000

International travel must be approved at least 10 business days before departure.

### 5.3 Emergency Travel
Emergency travel (less than 24 hours notice) must be approved verbally by the department head and confirmed in writing within 24 hours of travel commencement.

---

## SECTION 6 — EXPENSE SUBMISSION

### 6.1 Submission Timeline
All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of trip completion. Late submissions (30–60 days) require department head approval. Expenses submitted after 60 days will not be reimbursed.

### 6.2 Receipt Requirements
- Expenses above ₹500: Original receipt (digital acceptable) is mandatory
- Expenses below ₹500: Self-declaration permitted (maximum 3 per trip)
- GST invoices required for hotel stays and flights for company GST input credit

### 6.3 Expense Portal
All expenses must be submitted through the Yatra Expense Portal (RECAP). Paper-based submissions are no longer accepted as of FY2025. Expenses submitted outside the portal will not be processed.

### 6.4 Currency and FX
International expenses should be submitted in the original currency. The finance team will convert at the RBI reference rate for the date of expense. No FX markup is reimbursable.

---

## SECTION 7 — FLIGHT DISRUPTION ENTITLEMENTS

### 7.1 Delay Entitlements
- Delay of 2–4 hours: Meal voucher up to ₹400 (claim via airline or submit receipt)
- Delay exceeding 4 hours: Hotel accommodation if overnight becomes necessary, plus meals
- Delay exceeding 8 hours: Business class upgrade on alternative flight is permissible

### 7.2 Cancellation Entitlements
For airline-initiated cancellations: Full refund or next available flight at no extra cost (airline's obligation). Employees should not pay for alternative bookings without Yatra travel desk coordination.

### 7.3 Missed Flights
Missed flights due to employee error are NOT reimbursable. Missed flights due to traffic, weather, or documented emergencies require manager sign-off for rebooking costs.

### 7.4 Reporting Disruptions
All flight disruptions must be reported to the Yatra Travel Desk within 4 hours via the DIYA chat assistant or the helpline. This ensures proper tracking and entitlement processing.

---

## SECTION 8 — PROHIBITED EXPENSES

The following are never reimbursable under any circumstances:
- Alcohol and tobacco products
- Personal items (clothing, toiletries beyond travel essentials)
- Entertainment not related to business (movies, personal outings)
- Spouse or family travel add-ons
- Gym or spa charges (except when included in hotel package)
- Traffic fines, parking tickets, or penalties
- Expenses for travel deviations for personal reasons
- Luxury upgrades beyond policy limits

---

## SECTION 9 — COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT

### 9.1 Audit Process
Yatra Finance audits 20% of all expense reports on a random basis. Employees selected for audit must provide original receipts within 5 business days.

### 9.2 Policy Violations
First violation: Written warning and expense rejection.
Second violation: Expense rejection and reporting to HR.
Third violation: Disciplinary action as per HR policy.

Intentional fraud in expense reporting will result in immediate termination and may involve legal action.

### 9.3 Policy Updates
This policy is reviewed annually. Updates are communicated via HR email and reflected in the RECAP portal. Employees are responsible for staying current with policy versions.
