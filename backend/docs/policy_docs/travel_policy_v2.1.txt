YATRA CORPORATE TRAVEL POLICY — COMPLETE REFERENCE (v2.1, FY2025)

=== SECTION 1: FLIGHT POLICY ===

1.1 CLASS OF TRAVEL
All employees must travel economy class for domestic flights.
Business class is permitted only when the flight duration exceeds 6 hours or for C-suite (VP and above) employees on any route.
Premium economy is not reimbursable unless explicitly approved.

1.2 BOOKING LEAD TIME
Flights must be booked at least 3 business days before departure.
Last-minute bookings (under 3 days) require written approval from the reporting manager.
Emergency bookings (same day) require Department Head approval and must be justified in writing.

1.3 PREFERRED AIRLINES
Employees should book with preferred carriers in this order: Air India, IndiGo, Vistara.
Bookings on non-preferred carriers are allowed but must be cost-justified.

1.4 FARE DEVIATION
The booked fare must not exceed the lowest available fare by more than 20%.
Fare differences above 20% require manager approval and a written business justification.

1.5 CANCELLATIONS AND REFUNDS
All unused tickets must be cancelled at least 24 hours before departure to recover any refund.
Non-refundable tickets may be rebooked within 12 months; the rebooking fee is reimbursable.

=== SECTION 2: HOTEL POLICY ===

2.1 NIGHTLY RATE LIMITS
Tier-1 cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune): Maximum 6000 rupees per night.
Tier-2 cities (all other domestic locations): Maximum 4000 rupees per night.
International travel: Typical cap USD 150 per night.

2.2 PREFERRED HOTEL CHAINS
Marriott, ITC Hotels, Taj Hotels business rates apply automatically for Yatra employees.
Other hotels are permitted when preferred chains are unavailable or exceed the nightly cap.

2.3 LOYALTY POINTS
Employees may retain personal loyalty points earned on company-paid stays.
Points must not influence hotel selection in a way that violates the nightly rate cap.

=== SECTION 3: MEAL AND DAILY ALLOWANCE ===

3.1 DOMESTIC DAILY MEAL ALLOWANCE
Up to 800 rupees per day for all domestic travel.
Receipts required only for individual meals exceeding 300 rupees.

3.2 INTERNATIONAL DAILY MEAL ALLOWANCE
Up to 1500 rupees per day or equivalent in local currency.
Original receipts required for all amounts.

3.3 CLIENT ENTERTAINMENT
Maximum 1200 rupees per person for client meals.
Pre-approval required if the group exceeds 5 people or the total bill exceeds 10000 rupees.

3.4 ALCOHOL
Alcohol is strictly NOT reimbursable under any circumstances, including client entertainment.
Any meal receipt including alcohol must have the alcohol portion excluded from the claim.

3.5 ROOM SERVICE
Room service is reimbursable as part of the daily meal allowance.
Minibar charges are not reimbursable.

=== SECTION 4: GROUND TRANSPORT ===

4.1 TAXIS AND RIDE-HAILING
Uber and Ola cabs are reimbursable with a digital or printed receipt.
Surge pricing is reimbursable up to 2x the standard fare.

4.2 AUTO-RICKSHAWS
Reimbursable up to 200 rupees per trip. No receipt required below 100 rupees.

4.3 PERSONAL VEHICLE
Reimbursed at 8 rupees per kilometre for personal car usage.
Odometer readings start and end must be submitted.

4.4 AIRPORT TRANSFERS
Airport pickup and drop are reimbursable for all business trips.

4.5 METRO AND PUBLIC TRANSPORT
Fully reimbursable. No receipt required for amounts under 50 rupees.

=== SECTION 5: APPROVAL THRESHOLDS ===

5.1 SELF-APPROVED
Total trip cost under 15000 rupees: Employee can self-approve and proceed directly.

5.2 REPORTING MANAGER APPROVAL
Total trip cost 15000 to 50000 rupees: Requires email or portal approval from the direct reporting manager before booking.

5.3 DEPARTMENT HEAD APPROVAL
Total trip cost above 50000 rupees: Requires Department Head approval.
All last-minute bookings regardless of cost require Department Head approval.

5.4 INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL
All international travel regardless of cost requires Department Head approval plus HR sign-off.

=== SECTION 6: EXPENSE SUBMISSION ===

6.1 SUBMISSION DEADLINE
All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of trip completion or purchase date.
Late submissions 30 to 60 days require manager approval.
Submissions beyond 60 days will not be reimbursed without CFO exception.

6.2 RECEIPT REQUIREMENTS
Original receipt digital or paper required for all expenses above 500 rupees.
For expenses under 500 rupees a self-declaration is sufficient.

6.3 REIMBURSEMENT TIMELINE
Approved claims are processed within 7 business days.
Rejected claims are returned with a reason code within 3 business days.

=== SECTION 7: DISRUPTION AND EMERGENCIES ===

7.1 FLIGHT DELAY ENTITLEMENTS
Delays of 2 hours or more: Employee is entitled to a meal voucher up to 400 rupees reimbursable.
Delays of 4 hours or more: Employee may rebook on the next available flight at no personal cost.
Overnight delay: Hotel accommodation reimbursable up to 5000 rupees per night.

7.2 CANCELLATIONS BY AIRLINE
Full refund or next available flight at airline cost.
Additional hotel and meal costs caused by airline cancellation are fully reimbursable with receipts.

7.3 MISSED FLIGHTS
If the missed flight is due to employee negligence rebooking costs are not reimbursable.
If missed due to connecting flight delay or force majeure full rebooking support is provided.

=== SECTION 8: NON-REIMBURSABLE ITEMS ===

The following are explicitly NOT reimbursable under any circumstances:
Alcohol and tobacco products.
Personal entertainment including movies spa gym unless part of hotel mandatory package.
Minibar charges.
Traffic fines and parking violations.
Personal shopping.
Partner or spouse or family travel costs.
Trip insurance declined by the employee when offered.
